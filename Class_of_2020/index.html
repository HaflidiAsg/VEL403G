Class setup and a brief overview.

Okay, so what are we going to do?

The class subject is digital fabrication, and has been constructed from several FabAcademy modules. The course is hosted at the University of Iceland, but most practical lessons will be held at Fab Lab Reykjavik. The course is set up such that each student will need to document their own workload over the span of 14 weeks.

The student will have the opportunity to make their own portfolio outlinging their skills and capabilities. There are three mandatory modules that every student needs to do on their own. These are:

    Build a digital portfolio with html
    Design and make a parametric design of a contstruction kit
    3D print something that could not be made subtractively and 3D scan something with photogrammetry
 Additionally to the individual assignments, groups of three students each will need to work togeather making a final project that involves two chosen elements.
      Composites
      Molding and casting
      Large scale CNC milling of a pressfit design

    Nice! So how is each week set up?

    Each week we will have a lecture in class about themes outlined in the syllabus. Additionally, a random set of 10 students will need to present their individual work for the entire group every week. Sometimes the same student will need to present their work two weeks in a row, and sometimes some time will pass between presentations. The presentations are not supposed to be super structured, but the student should keep in mind that a review of their work might occur at any time so an organized webpage and a timely work schedule should be in order.
